### Foot Controls

1. **Accelerator**: Operated by the right foot, this control is crucial for speed adjustments. Remember, slight pressure can cause rapid speed increases, so practice is essential for smooth handling.

2. **Brake Pedal**: Also operated by the right foot. Apply more force to increase braking effort, especially in emergencies. Always avoid turning off the engine to stop the vehicle, as this can disable power-assisted brakes.

3. **Clutch Pedal**: For manual cars, engage with the left foot. It assists in gear changes and halts vehicle transmission. Automatic transmissions will only have accelerator and brake pedals for the right foot.

### Hand Controls

1. **Steering Wheel**: Hold at the "Quarter to Three" or "Ten to Two" positions for optimal control. Use the Pull-Push method while turning to maintain control and the ability to operate additional controls like signals and horns.

2. **Gear Shift Lever**:
   - *Manual Transmission*: Typically, there will be 5 forward and 1 reverse gear. Use gears to optimize engine performance and fuel efficiency.
   - *Automatic Transmission*: Utilize the "D" position for automatic gear changes as per the vehicle's programming.

3. **Parking Brake Lever**: Located between front seats, engage it when the vehicle is stopped for more than a few seconds to prevent rolling.

### Safety Precautions

- Always ensure to accommodate other road users and be aware of traffic signals and road signs.
- Avoid distractions like loud music and refrain from using mobile phones while driving.
- Practice the safe usage of controls to prevent hazards and ensure a smooth driving experience.
